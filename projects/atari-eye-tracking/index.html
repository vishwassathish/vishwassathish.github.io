Visualizing Gaze-Guided Attention in Atari

Write-up →

How do people actually look at Atari games — and does that change when they play better? This visualization explores human gaze patterns, fixation stability, and spatial attention across six games using the Atari-HEAD eye-tracking dataset, comparing normal play with high-score sessions.

Perceptual View
Foveated — Sharp center near the gaze point, blurred periphery. Shows how little of a frame peripheral vision actually processes. Resize the sharp region with Narrow / Medium / Wide.

Glimpses — Reveals which screen regions were sampled over the last N frames. Sharp patches at each gaze position fade over time; older fixations are dimmer.

Heatmap — Gaussian density of recent gaze positions as a warm color map (black → orange → white).

Focus / Window — Adjusts the sharp-region size (Foveated) or the history window length (Glimpses / Heatmap).

Gaze patterns across all recorded trials
Aggregation views:
  • Visible clip — how the metric evolves over the loaded 60-second clip. X-axis = time.
  • All trials — every recorded session pooled. Each dot = one trial. Diamond + bar = mean ± 1 SD.
  • Normal vs high-score — same dots split by skill level. Spot how expert players look differently.
Metrics:
  • Gaze path — how far eyes travel per minute (m/min). Higher = more scanning.
  • Focused gaze — % of 1-second windows where gaze stayed spatially compact. Higher = sustained focus.
  • Screen coverage — % of the 16×21 screen grid visited over the full trial. Higher = broader search.

Where do humans look when exploring for rewards?

Eye-tracking data from human players across three Atari games — Breakout, Montezuma's Revenge, and Space Invaders — reveals how attention shifts in the seconds before a reward event.

  • Gaze anticipates reward locations: players fixate on targets before points are scored, not after.
  • In Montezuma's Revenge (sparse rewards) gaze lingers broadly, suggesting exploratory search rather than reactive tracking.
  • In Breakout and Space Invaders (dense rewards) attention concentrates tightly on the active scoring zone 0–2 s before reward.
